Major Roles and Earnings

Hale’s most iconic role was Della Street in the television series Perry Mason. This role spanned from 1957 to 1966, with Hale appearing in 271 episodes. Her performance earned her an Emmy Award in 1959.

Additionally, she reprised this role in 30 made-for-TV movies from 1985 to 1995. The steady income from this long-running role significantly boosted her net worth.

Besides Perry Mason, Hale starred in numerous films, such as Higher and Higher and Jolson Sings Again, further adding to her earnings.

Awards and Recognitions

Hale’s talent did not go unnoticed. She won the Emmy Award for Best Supporting Actress in a Drama Series in 1959. In 1960, she was honored with a star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame.

These accolades not only cemented her status as a respected actress but also enhanced her marketability and financial worth.

Post-Perry Mason Career

After Perry Mason, Hale continued to act in both film and television. She appeared in popular shows like The Doris Day Show and Marcus Welby, M.D.

Hale also co-starred with her son, William Katt, in the 1978 film Big Wednesday. These roles kept her in the public eye and contributed to her ongoing earnings.
