Transition to Solo Career and First Major Hits

Josh’s decision to pursue a solo career was a turning point. He released his independent album, Changing Faces, in 2001, catching the attention of Hollywood Records, which signed him in 2002.

His first studio album, For the Ride Home, was released in 2003 and featured the hit single Amazing, propelling him into the spotlight. His follow-up album, Almost Honest, included the popular track Only You, solidifying his place in the music industry.

Expansion into Country Music

His debut country album, Georgia Clay, released in 2011, marked a new chapter in his career. The title track, Georgia Clay, became a top 20 hit on the country charts, showcasing his versatility as an artist.
