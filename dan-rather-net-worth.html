Dan Rather Finance Overview

The Early Life and Career Beginnings

He was born in Wharton, Texas, on October 31, 1931. He grew up in Houston, attending Lovett Elementary, Hamilton Middle School, and John H. Reagan High School.

After graduating from Sam Houston State University with a BA in Journalism, Rather began his career with the Associated Press. His early career also included roles at United Press, the Houston Chronicle, and several radio stations in Texas.

Rather’s breakthrough came in 1961 when he reported on Hurricane Carla, which led to the largest evacuation in U.S. history at that time. This significant coverage launched him into the national spotlight.

Major Career Milestones and Achievements

His career is marked by several key milestones. After joining CBS in 1962, he quickly rose through the ranks, becoming a national name. He covered significant events such as the assassination of President John F. Kennedy and the Watergate scandal.

In 1981, he succeeded Walter Cronkite as the anchor for the CBS Evening News, a position he held for 24 years. During his tenure, he conducted high-profile interviews with figures like Saddam Hussein and covered major global events, including the fall of the Berlin Wall and the Gulf War.

The Killian Documents Controversy

In 2004, he presented a report on 60 Minutes II that included documents critical of President George W. Bush’s service in the Texas Air National Guard.

The documents’ authenticity was quickly questioned, leading to a significant scandal. CBS eventually retracted the story, and Rather left the anchor desk in 2005.

This controversy marked a turning point in his career, leading to his departure from CBS in 2006.

Post-CBS Career and Ventures

After leaving CBS, Rather continued his career in media. He joined AXS TV, where he hosted Dan Rather Reports from 2006 to 2013.

This show allowed him to delve into investigative journalism, covering a wide range of topics. He also began hosting The Big Interview with Dan Rather, conducting in-depth interviews with celebrities and public figures.

Additionally, Rather has contributed to The Young Turks and writes a newsletter called Steady, where he shares his insights on current events.

Influence on Journalism

His influence on journalism is profound. His career has been a model of journalistic integrity and tenacity. He has received numerous awards, including several Peabody Awards and a lifetime achievement award at the Emmys.

Rather’s work has shaped modern news reporting, setting high standards for investigative journalism and broadcast news.

FAQs about Dan Rather

Who is Dan Rather?

Dan Rather is an American journalist and former news anchor for CBS Evening News. He’s known for his extensive career in journalism, covering significant events from the Vietnam War to the Watergate scandal.

What is he famous for?

He is famous for his long tenure as the anchor of CBS Evening News and his coverage of major historical events, including the assassination of JFK, the Civil Rights Movement, and the Iraq War.

What shows did Dan Rather host?

He hosted CBS Evening News, 60 Minutes, and 48 Hours. After CBS, he hosted Dan Rather Reports on HDNet and The Big Interview on AXS TV.

What controversies has he been involved in?

He was involved in a major controversy in 2004 over a report on President George W. Bush’s military service, which led to questions about the authenticity of the documents used and eventually resulted in his departure from CBS.

What is he doing now?

He is currently active on social media, especially Twitter, and he writes and produces content for his production company, News and Guts Media. He also hosts The Big Interview on AXS TV.

Has he written any books?

Yes, he has written several books, including his memoir Rather Outspoken: My Life in the News and What Unites Us: Reflections on Patriotism, which reflect his career and views on journalism.

What awards has Dan Rather won?

He has won numerous awards, including multiple Emmy Awards, a Peabody Award, and the Edward R. Murrow Lifetime Achievement Award. These accolades recognize his contributions to journalism.
